# SEARCH_CONSOLE_OBSERVATION_PLAN

## Sections to inspect

### Indexing > Pages

- watch `discovered` vs `indexed`
- note whether any important V10 pages remain discovered but not indexed
- watch whether low-value duplication appears where it should not

### Sitemaps

- confirm the current sitemap remains accepted
- do not resubmit sitemap unless a real material change happens later

### URL Inspection

- use for a small number of key pages only
- focus on `/about/`, `/topics/`, `/distinctions/`, `/ai-governance/`, `/local-first-ai/`, `/long-lived-ai-entities/`
- check canonical chosen by Google and last crawl timing when useful

### Enhancements / rich results when relevant

- watch breadcrumb-related signals
- note any profile/page rich-result hints if Google begins exposing them
- do not obsess over empty enhancement sections if traffic is still minimal

### Performance

- inspect impressions by page
- inspect impressions by query
- check whether glossary, distinctions, and topics begin to register impressions

## Signals to track

- discovered vs indexed
- canonical chosen by Google
- impressions by page
- impressions by query
- appearance of profile/page/breadcrumb signals
- whether `/glossary/`, `/distinctions/`, and `/topics/` begin to get impressions

## Frequency

- no more than once per day
- better one meaningful review per day than repeated refreshes with no new information

## Anti-noise rule

- do not resubmit URLs without material change
- do not inspect the same page repeatedly without new evidence
- do not treat zero movement over a short interval as a failure signal
- use notes, not memory, to compare changes across days
