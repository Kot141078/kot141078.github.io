Most AI economy talk is still pricing the wrong thing.

We keep acting as if the scarce resource is generation:

more output,
more speed,
more agents producing more text.

But generation is getting cheaper.

Verification is not.

And when generation becomes cheap, value does not stay where we expect.
It moves.

Not toward "better content,"
but toward artifacts that actually reduce uncertainty for someone else.

That is where I place the Economic Layer for Experience Artifacts.
Not around content.
Around experience that survived contact with reality.

An Experience Artifact is not economically meaningful because it exists.
Or because it sounds intelligent.

It matters when it carries:

- provenance,
- bounded scope,
- reviewable transfer conditions,
- and a consequence that remains after the answer ends.

But even that is not sufficient.

An Experience Artifact is something that can be:

- audited,
- replayed under constraint,
- and, if needed, refused.

If it cannot be challenged, it is not an artifact.
If it cannot be constrained, it is not safe to reuse.
If it cannot be replayed, it does not carry operational value.

This defines a different economy.
Not a bazaar of synthetic abundance,
but a discipline of selective circulation.

What can travel.
What must remain bounded.
What is too thin or too contaminated to reuse safely.

In real systems, a random note and a signed maintenance log are both "information."

But only one of them can stop a machine from failing next week.

The difference is not intelligence.
The difference is consequence.

One document survives reality.
The other does not.

That is why the second one costs more.

When generation becomes cheap, value migrates.
It moves toward artifacts that can be:

- verified,
- bounded,
- audited,
- and trusted after reality has taken its cut.

Not because they are persuasive,
but because they remain usable under constraint.

The next AI economy will not be built only on generation.

It will be built on artifacts that can be:

- inspected,
- challenged,
- replayed,
- and still hold.
