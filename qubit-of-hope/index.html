Positioning

Narrative layer, not protocol replacement

The book layer is a narrative companion and public entry into the world of c. It sits beside AGI, L4, AGL, and SER rather than replacing the canonical architecture layer.

Volume I remains the first narrative entry. Volume II and Volume III are confirmed continuation volumes with their own repositories, release surfaces, and language/format matrices.

What this is not

  • Not a substitute for the protocol layer.
  • Not the canonical home of AGI, L4, AGL, or SER definitions.
  • Not a claim that narrative and architecture are interchangeable.

Relation to adjacent layers

AGI provides the stack-level frame.

L4 and AGL keep feasibility and grounding constraints visible.

SER keeps continuity and sovereignty on the protocol side.
