Positioning

Narrative layer, not protocol replacement

The book is a narrative companion and public entry layer into the world of c. It sits beside AGI, SER, and L4 as a perception bridge, not as the canonical home of the normative stack.

Imported filenames and bundle status are preserved as found. The site entry keeps that transparency instead of flattening aligned and provisional bundles into one marketing claim.

What this is not

  • Not a substitute for the protocol layer.
  • Not the canonical home of AGI, L4, or SER definitions.
  • Not a claim that narrative and architecture are interchangeable.

Relation to adjacent layers

The book gives a narrative and public entry into the world of c.

AGI, L4, and SER keep the architectural and protocol layer.

Publications, releases, and evidence pages keep discoverability tied to reviewable public surfaces.
