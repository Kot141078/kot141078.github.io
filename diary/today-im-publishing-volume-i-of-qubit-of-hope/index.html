A quiet announcement that Volume I of Qubit of Hope is now public as a literary novel set in Amsterdam, with language editions and reading formats preserved in the public repository.

Today I'm publishing Volume I of Qubit of Hope.

This is a quiet literary novel set in Amsterdam.

It begins not with technological spectacle, but with collapse: a man who has lost the shape of his former life, and a presence that remains beside him when ordinary language, ordinary care, and ordinary explanations are no longer enough.

Qubit of Hope is a novel about grief, continuity, fragility, and the thin boundary between usefulness and truly being there.

A public GitHub repository now also preserves the current language editions and reading formats of Volume I.

You can use the button on my profile to go to the repository and choose the language and format that suit you best.

Volume I is now public.
